Mirrorless, DSLR, Camcorder

If you are just starting out, and are not completely sure about what to do, renting a camera is a lower cost way to make the content you want without having to commit nearly $1000 for a premiere film camera.

Starting out, the Panasonic LUMIX G7 is a good starter camera. It retails for about $600 to $750 dollars.

A higher performance option is the Canon EOS 80d at $1400 for retail.

You will also need a variety of lenses. Used lenses are often the best choice, as they can be had for much cheaper than buying them new. Renting can also be a possibility. Keep a lookout for lense swapping groups as well.

Microphones, Headphones, Audio Recorder

All of the options above will need a mic to capture audio, ideally on a pair of good headsets. Lavalier microphones can be clipped on, and are the cheapest way to get sound, but ideally you'd want a directional microphone, otherwise known as a shotgun microphone with a shockmount and wind screen.

Headset: Audio-Technica ATH-M30x for about $70

Microphone: Rode NTG2 can be had for about $270

Tripods are essential to steady shots
